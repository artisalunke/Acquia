Fields in custom reports¶

This feature is available only to Acquia Lift Starter - Premium or Acquia Lift - Premium subscribers. To upgrade your Acquia Lift subscription to this level, contact your Account Manager.

Using reports dashboards in Profile Manager

  • Overview
  • Details
  • Config
  • Custom
  • Fields

When building custom reports in Profile Manager, you can select from the following dimensions, or buckets of data, to search your website’s data warehouse in real time and display in your custom report.

After familiarizing yourself with the fields available in custom reports, you can create and customize your first report.

Detailed dimension list¶

Profile Manager groups dimensions by topic, based on the following list:

  • Content – Data related to visitors’ viewing of content on your website
  • Content UUID – UUID (Universal Unique Identifier) of content viewed on your website
  • Customer Site – Identifying information about the websites in your Acquia Lift subscription
  • Decision – Identifying information, content returned, and goals awarded from decisions
  • Event – Visitor events on your website
  • Event Custom Fields – Custom data fields for your website’s events
  • Event Date – Date information related to visitor events on your website
  • Event Engagement – Engagement scores for events on your website
  • External Campaign – UTM campaigns
  • Location – Location lookups of the originating IP address
  • Matched Segment – Data linking segments and events
  • Person – Your website’s visitors, including information about their locations, identities, and their preferences on your website
  • Person Custom Fields – Custom data fields for your website’s visitors
  • Person Date – Data about your visitors’ activities on your website
  • Person Engagement – Data relating to visitors’ engagement with your website
  • Person Identifier – Unique identifying information about your website’s visitors
  • Person link – Data linking multiple person IDs
  • Personalization – Data about the personalizations developed for your website
  • Segment – Segments matched to events on your website
  • Source – Data about the sources of touches
  • System Info – Data about the browser and platform associated with touches
  • Touch – Data about touches
  • Touch Custom Fields – Custom data about touches
  • Touch date – Date information for touches
  • Touch Engagement – Engagement scores associated with touches

Content¶

Name Description
Content ID The local Drupal content ID of the content displayed, or optionally another content ID if the content source is non-Drupal
Content Section The visitor context mapping configured in Drupal, typically mapped to the category or topic that the content relates to, such as telecommunications
Content Title The title of the page in a content view
Content Type The visitor context mapping configured in Drupal, typically mapped to the type of content, such as article
Keywords The visitor context mapping configured in Drupal, typically mapped to tags associated with the content, such as VendorA, VendorB
